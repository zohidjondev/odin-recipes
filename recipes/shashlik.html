Uzbek Shashlik: A Grilled Delight

Description

Shashlik, a popular grilled dish in Uzbek cuisine, consists of marinated meat skewered and cooked over an open flame. Traditionally made with lamb, but also with beef or chicken, the meat is marinated with onions, vinegar, and a blend of spices such as coriander, cumin, and black pepper. This process tenderizes the meat and infuses it with a rich flavor. Shashlik is typically enjoyed with fresh salads, bread, and a tangy dipping sauce.

Shashlik is more than just a dish; it's a social event. Cooking and eating shashlik is a communal activity often enjoyed during gatherings and celebrations. The smoky aroma of grilling meat, the convivial atmosphere, and the delicious taste of perfectly cooked shashlik make it a cherished part of Uzbek culinary tradition.

Ingredients

  • Lamb, beef, or chicken
  • Onions
  • Garlic
  • Vinegar
  • Vegetable oil
  • Cumin seeds
  • Coriander seeds
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Paprika

Preparing Process

  1. Meat: Cut the meat into bite-sized chunks.
  2. Marinade: In a large bowl, combine the meat with sliced onions, minced garlic, vinegar, vegetable oil, and spices (cumin, coriander, salt, black pepper, and paprika). Mix well to ensure all pieces are coated. Cover and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, preferably overnight.
  3. Skewers: Thread the marinated meat onto skewers, alternating with pieces of onion if desired.
  4. Grill: Preheat the grill to medium-high heat. Place the skewers on the grill and cook, turning occasionally, until the meat is cooked through and has a nice char on the outside (about 10-15 minutes).
  5. Serve: Serve the shashlik hot with fresh salads, bread, and a tangy dipping sauce.
