Uzbek Samsa: Savory Pastry Pockets

Description

Samsa is a beloved Uzbek pastry filled with a savory mixture of meat, onions, and spices. These flaky, golden pastries are typically baked in a tandoor oven, which gives them a unique texture and flavor. The filling usually consists of minced lamb or beef, finely chopped onions, and a blend of spices such as cumin and black pepper. Samsa is often enjoyed as a snack or appetizer and is perfect for sharing.

Samsa holds a special place in Uzbek cuisine, not only for its delicious taste but also for its cultural significance. It is commonly prepared for family gatherings, festivals, and celebrations. The process of making samsa is a labor of love, with the dough being carefully prepared and the fillings perfectly seasoned, making it a true representation of Uzbek hospitality and culinary expertise.

Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Butter or margarine
  • Minced lamb or beef
  • Onions
  • Cumin seeds
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
  • Egg (for egg wash)

Preparing Process

  1. Dough: In a large bowl, combine flour, water, and salt to form a soft dough. Knead until smooth. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Filling: In another bowl, combine minced meat, finely chopped onions, cumin seeds, black pepper, and salt. Mix well.
  3. Assembly: Roll out the dough on a floured surface and cut it into squares. Place a spoonful of filling in the center of each square. Fold the corners to the center and pinch to seal, forming a triangular or square shape.
  4. Egg Wash: Brush the tops of the samsa with beaten egg for a golden finish.
  5. Bake: Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the samsa on a baking sheet and bake for 25-30 minutes or until golden brown and cooked through.
  6. Serve: Serve hot, ideally with a side of fresh salad or yogurt sauce.
